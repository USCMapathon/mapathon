USC GIS Day

and Map-a-thon

Do you want to do more to immediately help people affected by recent natural disasters? Does the word “Bruin” ruin your day? Then join us with UCLA on November 14th for a special humanitarian map-a-thon and speaker event.

We will join forces (and compete!) with UCLA on GIS day. First, fellow students at USC and UCLA will share their GIS and mapping experiences through short lightning talks. Then we will have a friendly mapathon competition with UCLA.

Learn more about Open Street Map, Missing Maps, and how you can be part of the humanitarian mapping community!

When: Wednesday November 14th, 2018
Where: Ahmanson Lab, 3rd Floor of Leavey Library
Food will be provided!
Please RSVP!!!

Map-a-thon Toolkit

What to do before the Map-a-thon

  • Get an OSM account
  • This is the slideshow we will be using the day of the Ma-a-thon. If you can please go through it before you come!
  • Watch some more videos on how to use the task manager in Open Street Map.

    • What to expect once the Map-a-thon begins?

    If you are off-site please log-in to the Battle of Maps virtual room.

    For those participating virtually please go through the slideshow beforehand! It will be difficult to provide hands on help but we will definitely try!

    We will be picking projects from the HOTOSM website. We will be giving brief tutorials/workshops to get people up to speed and we will have volunteers on hand to get you started and answer questions. As we map we will have a leaderboard showing our progress against UCLA. In addition we will have a live feed so we can say hello and see how are counterparts are doing.

    USC Map-a-thon Team

    Andy Rutkowski is the Visualization Librarian at USC Libraries. He is interested in how mapping and spatial thinking can have an impact on a wide range of issues, including social justice, sustainability, and public policy.
    Abriannah Aiken is a 2nd year Architecture student minoring in Archaeology. She enjoys encouraging others to understand the complexities and injustices in the world and use privilege to create global impacts!
    Past Organizers
    Natalie Millstein is a senior Geodesign major with a minor in architecture. She enjoys investigating and evaluating ongoing change in the demographics and structures of urban environments.
    Precious Nwaoha is a Cognitive Science student in her final year. She is passionate about using data, technology, and people power to positively impact the world.
    Alex Chen is a senior studying GeoDesign and public health. He is interested in spatial analysis, disease surveillance, urban epidemiology, COER, and health policy, using GIS as an applied statistical method.
    Christian Lanec Relleve is a student of the USC School of Architecture minoring in HSGI (Human Security and Global Intelligence). He is interested in understanding territorial studies, defense infrastructures and the utilization to solve complex contextual problems using architecture.
